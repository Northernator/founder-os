# About Notion
## A story of tools and the future of work
![An illustration of several people working on computers at their desk](https://www.notion.com/_next/image?url=%2Ffront-static%2Fpages%2Fabout%2Fabout-01-v2.png&w=2048&q=75)
Hi there! If you’re reading this, you’re probably like me—spending most of your days in your office, in front of a computer.
![An illustration of a person leaving their chair and walking away](https://www.notion.com/_next/image?url=%2Ffront-static%2Fpages%2Fabout%2Fabout-02.png&w=2048&q=75)
You probably have fifteen tabs open: one for email, one for Slack, one for Google Docs, and on, and on…
![A photo of people working in a factory during the industrial revolution](https://www.notion.com/_next/image?url=%2Ffront-static%2Fpages%2Fabout%2Fabout-03%2Fen-US.png&w=2048&q=75)
As people flooded to factories during the Industrial Revolution, many tools were invented to ease the management overload.
![A photo of people working at a large computer in the mid 20th century](https://www.notion.com/_next/image?url=%2Ffront-static%2Fpages%2Fabout%2Fabout-04.png&w=2048&q=75)
Fast forward to the 1950s, when computers came into the workplace.
![An illustration of a person celebrating several documents](https://www.notion.com/_next/image?url=%2Ffront-static%2Fpages%2Fabout%2Fabout-05.png&w=2048&q=75)
Then something magical happened in the 1970s!
![An illustration of a person sitting on top of a video player](https://www.notion.com/_next/image?url=%2Ffront-static%2Fpages%2Fabout%2Fabout-06.png&w=2048&q=75)
![](https://www.notion.com/about)
Play

Please watch it on [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BFlop4sP8Os)
![](https://www.notion.com/_next/image?url=%2Ffront-static%2Fillustrated-icons%2FcatLookingUp.png&w=384&q=75)
![An illustration of type writers and filing cabinets with a person shrugging in confusion about these items](https://www.notion.com/_next/image?url=%2Ffront-static%2Fpages%2Fabout%2Fabout-07-v2%2Fen-US.png&w=2048&q=75)
And that’s where we are today.
![An illustration of a person taming a floating mess of documents](https://www.notion.com/_next/image?url=%2Ffront-static%2Fpages%2Fabout%2Fabout-08.png&w=2048&q=75)
That’s where Notion comes in. We want to break away from today’s tools—and bring back some of the ideas of those early pioneers.**all-in-one workspace**. Want a task list? A product roadmap? A design repository? They are now all in one place. You can even customize your own workspace from dozens of building blocks.
![An illustration of several people working at their desks in excitement and waving](https://www.notion.com/_next/image?url=%2Ffront-static%2Fpages%2Fabout%2Fabout-09.png&w=2048&q=75)
Hopefully you’ve gotten a good sense of why we started Notion. 
"**“ The best way to predict the future is to invent it.”**  
Alan Kay, computing pioneer"
